Chapter 7 Workshop Questions for Week 6

7.1 Copper complex question

Molecules A & B are diamidocarbene copper (I) complexes which have visible absorption spectra and are shown to phosphoresce.

Figure 7.1: The structures of copper complexes, A and B.

Figure 7.2: The absorbance spectra of copper complexes, A and B.

  • Suggest what absorbtion transitions are occurring within the molecules.

  • Suggest the origin of the moderately large molar extinction coefficient for the two chromophores.

  • When the concentration of B is increased in solution it shows a deviation from linearity in the absorbance calibration, in the same concentration range A does not show this, suggest why this may be the case.

Figure 7.3: The deviation of absorbance of copper complex B with increasing concentration

  • The absorption spectrum of A at 385 nm shows a red shift when in methanol. Explain the origin of this shift.

Both molecules have been shown to luminesce with lifetimes in the µs.

  • What is meant by the term lifetime?

  • What type of transition is occurring? Justify your answer.

  • For molecule B the lifetime in the solid state is 15.6 µs and 1.16 µs in DCM solution. Suggest why the difference between the two values.

  • If the quantum yield of emission (Φ_em_) in the solid state is 0.62 determine the quantum yield in DCM. What assumptions have you made?

  • Describe a method for how the quantum yield may be determined.

  • Emission occurs with lambda max at 636 nm. Suggest the origin of the large Stoke’s shift. (Absorption peaks are at 343 nm and 447 nm).

One use of emissive copper complexes is for use as oxygen sensors. At 20 ºC the quantum yield of emission of A is shown to be reduced from Φem = 0.65 in aerated DCM, to Φem = 0.50 in solution purged with oxygen. The lifetime was also shown to be reduced from 18 µs to 14 µs.

  • State why the quenching must be dynamic.

  • In oxygen saturated solution the concentration of dissolved oxygen is 1.4 mM. Determine the rate constant for the quenching of the excited state.

  • Is the reaction diffusion or activation controlled? Justify your answer. (η_DCM_ = 0.430 mPa s)
